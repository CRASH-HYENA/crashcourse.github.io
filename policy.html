Crash Course Weekend Convention Policy & Code of Conduct

1. Registration and Entry

Age Requirement: All attendees must be 18 years of age or older to attend the event. We may challenge anyone who appears to be under 25 years old, so please ensure you have a valid photo ID, such as a passport or driving license, on hand if requested.

Registration: Only those who have registered with Crash Course Weekend and hold a valid convention badge are permitted to attend the event. Badges must be visible at all times while on the convention grounds.

Ticket Transfers and Refunds: Tickets can be transferred to another person up until the registration period closes. Cancellations are eligible for a full refund within 7 days of payment; after this period, refunds will be handled on a case-by-case basis.

2. Code of Conduct

Respect and Behaviour: Attendees are expected to treat one another, staff, and venue personnel with respect. Any form of discrimination, harassment, or disruptive behaviour, including but not limited to racism, transphobia, and body shaming, will not be tolerated.

Consent and Physical Contact: Always ask for consent before engaging in any form of physical contact. Respect personal boundaries at all times.

Alcohol and Intoxication: Responsible alcohol consumption is expected. If an attendee becomes overly intoxicated or behaves aggressively, they may be asked to leave the event without a refund.

Fursuits and Costumes: Fursuits. pup hoods and costumes are encouraged. Please be mindful of your surroundings and ensure that your suit does not cause inconvenience or harm to others and be mindful of suiters having low visability.

3. Venue Guidelines

Accommodation: The event will be held in luxury eco-lodges. Attendees are responsible for maintaining their accommodations in good condition. Any damages to the eco-lodges or other property will be charged to the occupant.Low Nose hours are from 11pm to 7am.

Paid Attendance Policy: Only individuals who have paid and registered for Crash Course Weekend are allowed to attend and remain on-site. Anyone found on the premises without proper registration and payment will be removed and permanently banned from future events.

4. Health and Safety

Emergency Procedures: Familiarize yourself with the venueâ€™s emergency exits. In case of an emergency, follow staff instructions and evacuate the premises calmly.

Health Precautions: If you are feeling unwell during the event, notify a staff member. We encourage all attendees to take appropriate health precautions, particularly in group settings.

5. Accessibility

Support for Attendees with Disabilities: We strive to make our event accessible to all. Please notify us in advance if you have specific needs, such as accessible accommodation or priority seating at events.

6. Weapons and Dangerous Items

Prohibited Items: Real weapons are strictly prohibited. Any replica or prop weapons must be checked and approved by staff at registration. They will be tagged, and the tag must remain visible throughout the event.

Unsafe Items: Any item or substance that presents a serious risk to attendees, staff, or the venue will not be allowed.

7. Photography and Media

Consent for Photography: Please ask for consent before taking photos or videos of other attendees, especially during private or sensitive moments.

Social Media: Attendees are allowed to share their experiences on social media but are expected to respect the privacy of others. Do not share content that invades someone's privacy or could be considered harmful.

Event Photography: Crash Course Weekend staff may record and take photos during public events. By attending, you agree that your likeness may appear in promotional material for future events.
